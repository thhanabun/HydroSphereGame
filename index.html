    Construction and value hints

    Construction

    • Base Dam stores water as a reserve and slows discharge until water exceeds capacity.
    • Elevation Dam upgrades an existing dam so it can hold deeper water.
    • Conduit opens faster transfer routes and supplies irrigation to adjacent damp cells.
    • Powerhouse converts usable flow into hydropower income.

    Values

    • Land Tile is the main build surface for power and distribution structures.
    • Shore Tile connects land builds to nearby water and supports dams, conduits, and powerhouses.
    • Water Depth Tile represents channels or reservoir cells where depth matters most.
    • Credits pay construction costs and are gained from hydropower and irrigation.
    • Engineers limit how many build orders can be committed this turn.
    • Excavators/Mixers are locked until the construction job finishes.
    • Stored Water is your water reserve behind dams, measured as volume.
    • Headwater Sources refill upstream cells each committed turn; dry weather weakens them.
    • Hold turns mean the reserve must stay valid for several resolved turns.
    • Net Income is last turn income minus flood or drought penalties.
    • Hydrology details show water depth and flow for debugging basin behavior.
